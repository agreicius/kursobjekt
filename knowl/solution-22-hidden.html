Assemble the ingredients:

\begin{align*} \boldr'(t) \amp = (2\cos t, 1, -2\sin t)\\ \abs{\boldr'(t)} \amp= \sqrt{4\cos^2 t+4\sin^2t+1}=\sqrt{5} \text{.} \end{align*}

Now compute

\begin{align*} \int\limits_\mathcal{C}f(x,y,z)\, ds\amp = \int_0^{\pi}f(2\cos t, t, 2\sin t)\abs{\boldr'(t)}\, dt \amp(\knowl{./knowl/th_line_integral_scalar.html}{\text{2.1.6}})\\ \amp=\int_0^\pi (4\cos^2 t+t^2+4\sin^2 t)\sqrt{5}\, dt \\ \amp = \sqrt{5}\int_0^\pi 4+t^2\, dt\\ \amp = \sqrt{5}(4\pi+\pi^3/3)\text{.} \end{align*}
