Sage allows us to easily integrate over elementary regions via iterated integrals. As usual, the hard part is identifying the correct iterated integral to compute; Sage can then do the rest. Consider ExampleÂ 1.5.13, where we needed to compute \(\iiint\limits_\mathcal{D}1\, dV\text{.}\) The hard part was coming up with a suitable description of \(\mathcal{D}\text{:}\) e.g.,

\begin{equation*} D=\{(x,y,z)\colon -1\leq y\leq 1, y^2\leq x\leq 1, 0\leq 0\leq 1-x\}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Once this step is completed, the relevant triply iterated integral

\begin{equation*} \int_{-1}^1\int_{y^2}^1\int_0^{1-x}1\, dz\, dx\, dy \end{equation*}

is easy enough to compute.
