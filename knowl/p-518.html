Ingredients.

\begin{align*} \bolds_r \amp = \angvec{\cos\theta, \sin\theta, 1}\\ \bolds_\theta \amp= \angvec{-r\sin\theta, r\cos\theta, 0} \\ \bolds_r\times\bolds_\theta \amp =\angvec{-r\cos\theta, -r\sin\theta, r} \text{.} \end{align*}

Observe that since \(r\geq 0\text{,}\) the normal vector \(\bolds_r\times\bolds_\theta\) points upward, and hence toward the \(z\)-axis. Thus the outward orientation \(\boldn\) comes from \(\bolds_\theta\times\bolds_r\text{.}\)
