Item 2.

If \(\boldF(x_1,x_2,\dots, x_n)\) is the velocity vector of a fluid at the point \((x_1,x_2,\dots, x_n)\text{,}\) then \(\int_\mathcal{C}\boldF\cdot d\boldr\) is the flow along the path \(\boldr\) from \(\boldr(a)\) to \(\boldr(b)\text{.}\) The path \(\boldr\) is simple (one-to-one except possibly at the endpoints) and closed (\(\boldr(a)=\boldr(b)\)), then \(\int_\mathcal{C}\boldF\cdot d\boldr\) is called the circulation around \(\mathcal{C}\text{.}\)
