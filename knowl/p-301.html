Let \(R=[0,1]\times [0,1]\text{.}\) It is clear that \(f(x,y)\geq 0\) for all \((x,y)\text{.}\) Furthermore, we have

\begin{align*} \iint\limits_{\R^2}f(x,y)\, dA \amp = \iint\limits_{R}f(x,y)\, dA \amp ((x,y)\notin R\implies f(x,y)=0)\\ \amp=\int_0^1\int_0^16x^2y\, dy\, dx \\ \amp =6\int_0^1x^2\, dx\int_0^1 y\, dy\\ \amp =6(1/3)(1/2)=1\text{.} \end{align*}
