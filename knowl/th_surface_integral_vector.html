Theorem 2.6.18. Surface integral of vector field.

Let \(\boldr\colon \mathcal{R}\rightarrow \mathcal{S}\) be a smooth parametrization of the orientable surface \(\mathcal{S}\text{,}\) let \(\boldF\) be a vector field that is continuous on \(\mathcal{S}\text{,}\) and let \(\boldn\colon \mathcal{S}\rightarrow \R^3\) be the orientation determined by \(\frac{\boldr_u\times\boldr_v}{\abs{\boldr_u\times \boldr_v}}\text{.}\) We have

\begin{equation} \iint\limits_\mathcal{S}\boldF\cdot \boldn\, d\sigma= \iint\limits_{\mathcal{R}}\boldF\cdot (\boldr_u\times \boldr_v)\, dA\text{.}\tag{2.6.2} \end{equation}

Furthermore letting \(-\boldn\) be the opposite orientation of \(\boldn\text{,}\) we have

\begin{equation} \iint\limits_\mathcal{S}\boldF\cdot (-\boldn)\, d\sigma= \iint\limits_{\mathcal{R}}\boldF\cdot (\boldr_v\times \boldr_u)\, dA= - \iint\limits_\mathcal{S}\boldF\cdot \boldn\, d\sigma\text{.}\tag{2.6.3} \end{equation}
