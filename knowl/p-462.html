Assume \(\mathcal{S}\) is the union of smoothly parametrized surfaces \(\mathcal{S}_1, \mathcal{S}_2,\dots \mathcal{S}_n\) whose interiors do not intersect. If \(f\) is a continuous function defined on \(\mathcal{S}\text{,}\) we define the surface integral of \(f\) over \(S\) as

\begin{equation*} \iint\limits_\mathcal{S}f\, d\sigma=\sum_{k=1}^n \iint\limits_{S_k}f\, d\sigma\text{.} \end{equation*}
