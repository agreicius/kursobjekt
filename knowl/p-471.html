Assume \(\mathcal{S}\) is a smooth orientable surface with orientation \(\boldn\colon \mathcal{S}\rightarrow\R^3\) and parametrization \(\boldr(u,v)\text{.}\) We have the following facts:

  1. There are exactly two orientations on \(\mathcal{S}\text{:}\) namely, the given orientation \(P\mapsto \boldn(P)\) and its opposite \(P\mapsto -\boldn(P)\text{.}\)

  2. The function

    \begin{equation*} P=\boldr(u_0,v_0)\mapsto \frac{\boldr_u\times \boldr_v(u_0,v_0)}{\abs{\boldr_u\times\boldr_v(u_0,v_0)}} \end{equation*}

    is one orientation on \(\mathcal{S}\text{;}\) the function

    \begin{equation*} P=\boldr(u_0,v_0)\mapsto \frac{\boldr_v\times \boldr_u(u_0,v_0)}{\abs{\boldr_u\times\boldr_v(u_0,v_0)}} \end{equation*}

    is the other.
