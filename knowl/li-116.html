Item 2.

If \(\boldr, \bolds\) both traverse \(\mathcal{C}\) exactly once, but \(\bolds\) starts where \(\boldr\) ends, and vice-versa, then we call the parametrization \(\bolds\) a reverse of \(\boldr\text{.}\) Denote by \(\mathcal{C}\) the curve as parametrized by \(\boldr\text{,}\) \(-\mathcal{C}\) the curve as parametrized by the reverse path \(\bolds\text{.}\) We have

\begin{equation*} \int_{-\mathcal{C}}\boldF\cdot d\boldr=-\int_\mathcal{C}\boldF\cdot d\boldr\text{.} \end{equation*}
