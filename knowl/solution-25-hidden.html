First we convert the equation \(z=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\) defining the cone into spherical coordinates with the help of the auxiliary equations (1.8.4)â€“(1.8.5):

\begin{align*} z=\sqrt{x^2+y^2} \amp \iff \frac{z}{r}=1\\ \amp\iff \tan\phi=1 \\ \amp \iff \phi=\pi/4\text{.} \end{align*}

We see that for a point to lie within the cone \(z=\sqrt{x^2+y^2}\) we need \(0\leq \phi\leq \pi/4\text{.}\) Next to lie within the sphere, we need \(0\leq \rho\leq R\text{.}\) Since there is no condition on \(\theta\text{,}\) the spherical description of \(D\) is

\begin{equation*} \{(\rho, \phi,\theta)\colon 0\leq \theta\leq 2\pi, 0\leq \phi\leq \pi/4, 0\leq \rho\leq R\}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Now compute

\begin{align*} \operatorname{vol} D \amp=\iiint\limits_D 1\, dV \\ \amp = \int_0^{2\pi}\int_0^{\pi/4}\int_0^{R}\rho^2\sin\phi\, d\rho\, d\phi\, d\theta \amp \text{(spher. coord.)}\\ \amp =2\pi\int_0^{\pi/4}\sin\phi\, d\phi\int_0^R\rho^2\, d\rho \\ \amp =2\pi\left(1-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)\left(\frac{1}{3}R^3\right)\\ \amp = \frac{2}{3}\pi R^3\left(1-\frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}\right)\text{.} \end{align*}
