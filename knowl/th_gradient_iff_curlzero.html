Theorem 2.3.16. Gradient iff zero curl (connected and simply connected domain).

Let \(D\subseteq \R^n\) be open, connected and simply connected. If the component functions of \(\boldF\colon \R^n\rightarrow \R^n\) have continuous partial derivatives on \(D\) then \(\boldF=\nabla f\) if and only if \(\curl\times \boldF=\boldzero\text{.}\) Using logical shortand:

\begin{equation} \boldF=\nabla f\iff \nabla\times \boldF=\boldzero\text{.}\tag{2.3.8} \end{equation}

Note: if \(n=2\text{,}\) we understand \(\nabla\times \boldF=\boldzero\) to mean \(\partial F_2/\partial x-\partial F_1/\partial y=0\text{.}\)
