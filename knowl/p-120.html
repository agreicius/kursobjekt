An elementary planar region (of type 1 or type 2) is a subset \(\mathcal{R}\subseteq \R^2\) of one of the following forms.

  • Type 1.

    We have

    \begin{equation*} \mathcal{R}=\{(x,y)\colon a\leq x\leq b, g_1(x)\leq y\leq g_2(x)\}\text{,} \end{equation*}

    where \(g_1, g_2\) are continuous on \([a,b]\text{.}\)

  • Type 2.

    We have

    \begin{equation*} \mathcal{R}=\{(x,y)\colon h_1(y)\leq x\leq h_2(y), c\leq y\leq d\}\text{,} \end{equation*}

    where \(h_1, h_2\) are continuous on \([c,d]\text{.}\)
