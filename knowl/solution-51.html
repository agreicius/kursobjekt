Solution 2.6.8.1.

We have \(\mathcal{T}=T_1\cup T_2\cup T_3\cup T_4\text{,}\) where the \(T_k\) are the four triangular faces of \(\mathcal{T}\text{,}\) and thus

\begin{equation*} \iint\limits_\mathcal{T}f\, d\sigma=\iint\limits_{T_1}f\, d\sigma+\iint\limits_{T_2}f\, d\sigma+\iint\limits_{T_3}f\, d\sigma+\iint\limits_{T_4}f\, d\sigma\text{.} \end{equation*}

Let \(T_1, T_2\) be the triangular faces lying in the coordinate planes \(x=0\) and \(y=0\text{,}\) respectively. Since \(f(x,y,z)=0\) for all points in these two surfaces, the corresponding surface integrals \(\iint_{T_k}f\, d\sigma\text{,}\) \(k=1,2\text{,}\) are equal to zero. Thus we need only compute the integrals of \(f\) over \(T_3\) and \(T_4\text{.}\)

  • Integral over \(T_3\).

    Let \(T_3\) be the face of the tetrahedron in the \(xy\)-plane. We parametrize as

    \begin{align*} \boldr(x,y) \amp =(x,y,0)\\ \mathcal{R}_1\colon 0\amp\leq x\leq 1, 0\leq y\leq 1-x \text{.} \end{align*}

    A simple computation shows \(\abs{\boldr_x\times \boldr_y}=1\text{.}\) Thus

    \begin{align*} \iint\limits_{T_1}f\, d\sigma\amp =\int_0^1\int_0^{1-x}xy\, dy\, dx\\ \amp =\frac{1}{2}\int_0^1x(1-x)^2\, dx \\ \amp =-\frac{1}{2}\int_1^0 (1-u)u^2\, du \amp (u=1-x, du=-dx)\\ \amp =\frac{1}{2}(1/3-1/4)=\frac{1}{24} \end{align*}
  • Integral over \(T_4\).

    Let \(T_4\) be triangle with vertices \((1,0,0), (1,0,0),(0,1,0)\text{.}\) This is the region of the plane \(z=x+y\) lying over the planar triangle with vertices \((0,0,0), (1,0,0), (0,1,0)\text{.}\) Since \(z\) is expressed as a function of \(x,y\) we parametrize easily as

    \begin{align*} \bolds(x,y) \amp =(x,y,x+y)\\ \mathcal{R}_{2}\colon 0 \amp\leq x\leq 1, 0\leq y\leq 1-x \text{.} \end{align*}

    Again, a straightforward computation shows \(\abs{\bolds_x\times \bolds_y}=\sqrt{3}\text{,}\) and thus

    \begin{align*} \iint\limits_{T_4}f\, d\sigma\amp =\iint\limits_{\mathcal{R}_2}f(\bolds(x,y))\sqrt{3}\, dA \amp \\ \amp= \sqrt{3}\int_0^1\int_0^{1-x}xy\, dy\, dx \\ \amp =\frac{\sqrt{3}}{24}\text{,} \end{align*}

    where we use our work from above for the last equality.

In conclusion we have
\begin{equation*} \iint\limits_{\mathcal{T}}=\iint\limits_{T_1}f\, d\sigma+\iint\limits_{T_2}f\, d\sigma =\frac{\sqrt{3}+1}{24}\text{.} \end{equation*}
