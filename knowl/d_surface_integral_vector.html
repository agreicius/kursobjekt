Definition 2.6.16. Surface integral of vector field.

Let \(\mathcal{S}\) be a smooth orientable surface, and let \(\boldF\) be a vector field that is continuous on \(\mathcal{S}\text{.}\) We define the surface integral of \(\boldF\) over \(\mathcal{S}\) with respect to the orientation \(\boldn\colon \mathcal{S}\rightarrow \R^3\) as the scalar surface integral

\begin{equation*} \iint_\mathcal{S}\boldF\cdot \boldn\, d\sigma\text{.} \end{equation*}

This integral is also called the flux of \(\boldF\) across \(\mathcal{S}\) with respect to the orientation \(\boldn\text{.}\)
