More generally, if \(\mathcal{S}\) is a smooth surface that is the boundary of a bounded solid region \(D\subseteq\R^3\text{,}\) then \(\mathcal{S}\) has both an outward and inward orientation. In particular, all such surfaces are orientable. Another example of such a surface is the torus, which is the boundary surface of a solid doughnut.
