Definition 2.6.1. Surface integral of scalar function.

Let \(\boldr\colon \mathcal{R}\rightarrow \R^3\) be a smooth parametrization of the surface \(\mathcal{S}\text{,}\) and let \(f\) be a continuous function defined on \(\mathcal{S}\text{.}\) The surface integral of \(f\) over \(\mathcal{S}\) is defined as

\begin{equation*} \iint\limits_\mathcal{S}f(x,y,z)\, d\sigma=\iint\limits_{\mathcal{R}}f(\boldr(u,v))\abs{\boldr_u\times \boldr_v}\, dA\text{.} \end{equation*}
