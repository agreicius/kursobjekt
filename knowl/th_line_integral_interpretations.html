Theorem 2.1.8. Interpretations.

Assume \(\boldr\colon [a,b]\rightarrow \R^n\) is a smooth parametrization of \(\mathcal{C}\) and that \(\boldr\) is one-to-one on \([a,b]\text{,}\) except potentially at the endpoints (i.e., “\(\boldr\) traverses \(\mathcal{C}\) exactly once”).

  1. The line integral \(\int_\mathcal{C} 1\, ds\) computes the arc length of \(\mathcal{C}\) between \(P=\boldr(a)\) and \(Q=\boldr(b)\text{.}\)

  2. If \(n=2\) and \(f(x,y)\geq 0\) is continuous on \(\mathcal{C}\text{,}\) then \(\int_\mathcal{C}f(x,y)\, ds\) computes the area of the “curtain” over \(\mathcal{C}\) with heights given by \(f(x,y)\text{.}\)

  3. If \(\delta(x_1,x_2,\dots, x_n)\geq 0\) is a continuous mass density on \(\mathcal{C}\text{,}\) where \(\mathcal{C}\) is treated as an infinitely thin wire, then \(\int_\mathcal{C}\delta(x_1,x_2,\dots, x_n)\, ds\) computes the mass of \(\mathcal{C}\text{.}\)
