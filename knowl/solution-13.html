Solution 1.4.5.1.

Observe that the curves \(y=1-x^2\) and \(y=x^2-1\) intersect at the points \((1,0)\) and \((-1,0)\text{,}\) and that we have \(x^2-1\leq 1-x^2\) for \(x\in [-1,1]\text{.}\) We describe \(\mathcal{R}\) as a type-1 region:

\begin{equation*} \mathcal{R}=\{(x,y)\colon -1\leq x\leq 1, x^2-1\leq y\leq 1-x^2\text{.} \end{equation*}

First compute the area of \(\mathcal{R}\text{:}\)

\begin{align*} \operatorname{area}\mathcal{R} \amp=\iint\limits_{\mathcal{R}}1\, dA \\ \amp=\int_{-1}^1\int_{x^2-1}^{1-x^2}1\, dy\, dx \\ \amp = \int_{-1}^12(1-x^2)\, dx\\ \amp =\frac{8}{3}\text{.} \end{align*}

Now compute

\begin{align*} \operatorname{avg}_{\mathcal{R}} T \amp=\frac{1}{\operatorname{area}\mathcal{R}}\iint\limits_{\mathcal{R}} x^2+1\, dA \\ \amp=\frac{3}{8}\int_{-1}^1\int_{x^2-1}^{1-x^2}x^2+1\, dy\, dx \\ \amp = \frac{3}{8}\int_{-1}^12x^2(1-x^2)+2(1-x^2)\, dx\\ \amp = \frac{3}{4}\int_{-1}^1 2(1-x^2)(1+x^2)\, dx\\ \amp =\frac{3}{2}(x-\frac{1}{5}x^5)\Bigr\vert_{0}^1\\ \amp = \frac{6}{5} \text{ degrees celcius}\text{.} \end{align*}
