Definition 2.6.7. Surface integral for piecewise smooth surfaces.

A piecewise smooth surface is the union of smoothly parametrized surfaces whose interiors are non-overlapping, and whose intersections form piecewise smooth curves. If \(\mathcal{S}=\mathcal{S}_1\cup\mathcal{S}_2\cdots\cup\mathcal{S}_n\) is piecewise smooth, and if \(f\) is continuous on \(\mathcal{S}\text{,}\) we define the surface integral of \(f\) over \(S\) as

\begin{equation*} \iint\limits_\mathcal{S}f\, d\sigma=\sum_{k=1}^n \iint\limits_{S_k}f\, d\sigma\text{.} \end{equation*}
