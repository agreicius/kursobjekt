If \(\boldr(t)=(g_1(t),g_2(t))\) is a parametrization of \(\mathcal{C}\) with counterclockwise (positive) orientation, then the outward unit normal vector can be expressed as

\begin{equation*} \boldn(t)=\frac{\angvec{g_2'(t),-g_1'(t)}}{\sqrt{g_1^2(t)+g_2^2(t)}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

Furthermore, if \(\boldF(x,y)=\angvec{F_1(x,y),F_2(x,y)}\text{,}\) then

\begin{align*} \oint_\mathcal{C}\boldF\cdot \boldn \, ds\amp = \int_a^b\angvec{F_1(\boldr(t)),F_2(\boldr(t))}\cdot \angvec{g_2'(t),-g_1'(t)}\, dt\\ \amp = \int_a^b F_1(g_1(t),g_2(t))g_2'(t)-F(g_1(t),g_2(t))g_1'(t)\, dt\\ \amp = \oint_\mathcal{C} F_1\, dy -F_2\, dx\text{.} \end{align*}
