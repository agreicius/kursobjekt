We have

\begin{align*} \int_\mathcal{C}\boldF\cdot d\boldr \amp = \int_0^{2\pi}\boldF(\boldr(t))\cdot \boldr'(t)\, dt\\ \amp =\int_0^{2\pi}\boldF(\cos t, \sin t, t)\cdot \angvec{-\sin t, \cos t, 1}\, dt \\ \amp =\int_0^{2\pi}\angvec{-\sin t, t,2\cos t}\cdot \angvec{-\sin t, \cos t, 1}\, dt\\ \amp =\int_0^{2\pi}\sin^2 t+t\cos t+2\cos t\, dt\\ \amp = \pi\text{.} \end{align*}

(To perform the integration in the penulitimate line, use the double-angle formula \(\sin^2 t=\frac{1}{2}(1-\cos 2\t)\text{,}\) and integration by parts.)
