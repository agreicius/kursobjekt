Let \(\boldF\) be a vector field on the set \(D\subseteq\R^n\text{.}\) We use the abbreviations ‘o’, ‘c’, ‘sc’ for open, connected, and simply connected. For statements involving curl, assume the partial derivatives of the component functions of \(\boldF\) are continuous.

\begin{equation*} \begin{array}{rccccc} D \text{ is o}\colon \amp \boldF \operatorname{ conservative} \amp \impliedby \amp \boldF=\nabla f\amp \implies\amp \nabla\times \boldF=\boldzero. \\ D \text{ is o+c}\colon \amp \boldF \operatorname{ conservative} \amp \iff \amp \boldF=\nabla f\amp \implies\amp \nabla\times \boldF=\boldzero. \\ D \text{ is o+c+sc}\colon \amp \boldF \operatorname{ conservative} \amp \iff \amp \boldF=\nabla f\amp \iff\amp \nabla\times \boldF=\boldzero. \end{array} \end{equation*}

Additionally, for any domain \(D\) we always have the following loop property equivalence:

\begin{equation*} \boldF \ \operatorname{ conservative}\iff \oint_\mathcal{C}\boldF\cdot d\boldr=0\text{.} \end{equation*}
