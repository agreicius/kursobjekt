Definition 2.2.12. Flux.

Let \(\boldr=(g_1(t),g_2(t))\text{,}\) \(a\leq t\leq b\) be a smooth simple closed parametrization of the curve \(\mathcal{C}\subseteq\R^2\text{,}\) and assume \(\boldF=(F_1,F_2)\) is continuous on \(\mathcal{C}\text{.}\) For all \(a\leq t\leq b\text{,}\) the unit normal vector to \(\mathcal{C}\) with positive orientation is defined as

\begin{equation*} \boldn(t)=\frac{\angvec{g_2'(t),-g_1'(t)}}{\sqrt{g_1^2(t)+g_2^2(t)}}\text{.} \end{equation*}

The flux of \(\boldF\) across \(\mathcal{C}\) is defined as

\begin{align*} \operatorname{Flux}_\mathcal{C} \boldF \amp =\int_\mathcal{C}\boldF\cdot \boldn \, ds \\ \amp=\int_a^b\boldF(\boldr(t))\cdot \boldn(t) \abs{\boldr'(t)}\, dt \\ \amp = \int_a^b F_1(\boldr(t))g_2'(t)-F_2(\boldr(t))g_1'(t)\, dt\\ \amp = \oint_\mathcal{C} F_1\, dy-F_2\, dx\text{.} \end{align*}
