Let \(\boldF(x,y)=(xy,x+y)\text{.}\)

  1. Compute the integral of \(\boldF\) along the segment \(\mathcal{C}\) of the curve \(x=y^2\) that starts at \((1,-1)\) and ends at \((4,2)\text{.}\)

  2. Compute the integral of \(\boldF\) along the reverse \(-\mathcal{C}\) of the path in (1): i.e., the segment of the curve \(x=y^2\) that starts at \((4,2)\) and ends at \((1,-1)\text{.}\)
