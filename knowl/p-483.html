More generally, if \(\mathcal{S}\) is a smooth surface that is the boundary of a bounded solid region \(D\subseteq\R^3\text{,}\) then \(\mathcal{S}\) has both an outward and inward orientation. In particular, all such surfaces are orientable. The sphere discussed above is one such surface: it is the boundary of the solid ball \(x^2+y^2+z^2\leq 1\text{.}\) Another example is the torus: the boundary surface of a solid doughnut.
