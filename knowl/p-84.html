Consider the rectangle \(R=[a,b]\times [c,d]\subseteq \R^2\text{:}\) i.e.,

\begin{equation*} R=\{(x,y)\in \R^2\colon a\leq x\leq b, c\leq y\leq d\}\text{.} \end{equation*}

A partition \(P\) of the rectangle \(R\) is a subdivision \(R=R_1\cup R_2\cdots R_n\) of \(R\) into smaller subrectangles \(R_k\) using a grid of lines parallel to the \(x\)- and \(y\)-axes. The norm of \(P\text{,}\) denoted \(\norm{P}\text{,}\) is defined as the maximum width or length of any of the subrectangles of \(P\text{.}\) A pointed partition is a partition \(P\) along with a choice of sample points \((x_k, y_k)\in R_k\) lying in each subrectangle.
