Section 1.1 Double integrals and iterated integrals over rectangles

Definition 1.1.1. Rectangles and partitions.

Consider the rectangle \(R=[a,b]\times [c,d]\subseteq \R^2\text{:}\) i.e.,

\begin{equation*} R=\{(x,y)\in \R^2\colon a\leq x\leq b, c\leq y\leq d\}.\text{.} \end{equation*}

A partition \(P\) of the rectangle \(R\) is a subdivision of \(R\) into smaller rectangles using a grid of lines parallel to the \(x\)- and \(y\)-axes. The norm of \(P\text{,}\) denoted \(\norm{P}\text{,}\) is defined as the maximum width or length of any of the subrectangles of \(P\text{.}\)

Definition 1.1.2. Riemann sums in two variables.

Let \(f\) be a function of two variables defined on the rectangle \(R=[a,b]\times [c,d]\text{.}\) Let \(P\) be a partition of \(R\text{,}\) let \(R_1,R_2,\dots, R_n\) be the distinct subrectagles of \(R\text{,}\) let \(\Delta A_k\) be the area of subrectangle \(R_k\text{,}\) and for each \(1\leq k\leq n\) let \((x_k, y_k)\) be a chosen point of subrectangle \(R_k\text{.}\) The Riemann sum \(S\) associated to this choice of partition and points \((x_k,y_k)\) is defined as

\begin{equation} S=\sum_{k=1}^nf(x_k,y_k)\Delta A_k\text{.}\tag{1.1.1} \end{equation}
